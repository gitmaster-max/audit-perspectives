One audit question.
Every stakeholder who has to live with the answer.

A single view of an audit question returns the answer from whoever you asked. A Partner and a Staff auditor on the same engagement are looking at the same risk with completely different information, incentives, and accountability. Audit Perspectives runs the question through all of them at once — and maps where they disagree.

The method

Audit Perspectives adapts Stanford OVAL Lab's STORM method (Shao et al., NAACL 2024) — multi-perspective question asking as a research technique — into an audit-specific framework. Four stages, always in order.

  1. 01

    Topic analysis

    Classify the question — technical accounting, audit procedure, risk assessment, control, quality/independence, or regulatory — and name the industry context.

  2. 02

    The perspective grid

    Write a distinct view from every role that sees the question differently. Collapse roles that see it identically — no padding, no empty distinctions.

  3. 03

    The tension map

    Table out where perspectives actively conflict: who disagrees, what it's actually about, whether it's operational, risk, regulatory, or incentive-driven — and which tension is load-bearing.

  4. 04

    Synthesis

    What the full team agrees on, where it divides and why, the regulatory exposure, the load-bearing question, and a practical recommendation specific enough to act on.

Segments

Core Audit and the external layer run on every question. IT Audit, Tax, and Advisory are pulled in when the topic touches their territory.

SegmentRolesRuns when
Core Audit Staff → Senior → Manager → Senior Manager → Executive Director → Partner → Independent Reviewer → Quality Reviewer (EQCR) Always
IT Audit Staff → Partner Automated controls, ITGCs, system-generated evidence, data integrity, ERP change, data analytics
Tax Staff → Partner ASC 740, uncertain tax positions, transfer pricing, valuation allowances, tax reform impacts
Advisory Staff → Partner ICFR design/effectiveness, process risk, ERM, operational findings with audit implications
External PCAOB Inspection · AICPA Peer Review · End Financial Users · Activist Investors · Sector Regulators Always — regulators selected by sector (e.g. NAIC / CMS for health insurance)

Sample run

"How should we approach the risk assessment for IBNR reserves at a health insurer?"

Staff / Senior

The actuarial workpapers reconcile, but the completion factors for the most recent three months rely almost entirely on the actuary's judgment — there isn't enough paid claims history yet to test them independently. We can document the reconciliation, but we can't independently verify the tail without more runout.

Manager

The question is whether our reliance on the specialist's methodology is adequate given how sensitive IBNR is to the completion factor assumption in the most recent periods. I need the sensitivity analysis to show what a reasonable range of assumptions does to the reserve before I'm comfortable with the approach.

Partner

IBNR is almost always the largest, least-verifiable estimate on a health insurer's balance sheet, and it's exactly where a PCAOB inspector will look first. My exposure isn't the reconciliation — it's whether the file shows we independently challenged management's assumptions rather than just checked the specialist's math.

Sector Regulator — NAIC

Statutory reserve adequacy is a solvency question, not just a GAAP question. A reserve that is technically supportable under GAAP can still trigger a regulatory finding if it sits at the aggressive end of a reasonable range during a period of adverse claims development.

Load-bearing tension

Staff has flagged — informally — that the most recent completion factors are judgment-heavy with thin data support. That concern hasn't reached the Partner's sign-off conversation. The gap between what Staff knows about the data and what's been escalated is where the audit deficiency risk actually concentrates.

Install

Audit Perspectives is a Claude Code skill. Drop the skill file into your skills directory and invoke it from any conversation.

# macOS / Linux
mkdir -p ~/.claude/skills/audit-perspectives
curl -o ~/.claude/skills/audit-perspectives/SKILL.md \
  https://raw.githubusercontent.com/gitmaster-max/audit-perspectives/main/SKILL.md

# then, inside Claude Code
/audit-perspectives "How should we approach the risk assessment for IBNR reserves at a health insurer?"
